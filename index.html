03  ·  The Logic

How I think
about delivery.

01
5W
Root Cause Focus
I solve the machine,
not the output.
Every recurring delivery problem is a signal that the underlying system was never actually fixed — it was managed around. I use 5 Whys analysis to cut through the symptoms and find the structural failure point. Then I fix that.
"You can't patch your way to predictability."
02
PK
Poka-Yoke Design
Make the right path
the easy path.
Teams don't drift into bad habits because they're careless. They drift because the environment makes it easier to go wrong than right. I configure delivery systems so that correct behaviour is the path of least resistance. No enforcement needed.
"Good systems make bad outcomes difficult."
03
RS
Result-Oriented Silence
If you need a meeting
to know the status, I've failed.
I measure success by what stops happening — no status meetings, no chasing engineers for answers, no leadership flying blind. When the system is well-built, visibility is automatic. Silence means it's working.
"The best reporting system is invisible."
04
SL
Structural Logic
Organisations
obey physics.
My Mechanical Engineering background gives me a lens most delivery leads don't have. I see teams the way an engineer sees a machine — thermodynamics, kinematics, energy loss. Every blocked team has a choke point. Every slow process has friction that can be engineered out.
"Every system has a critical path. Find it."
